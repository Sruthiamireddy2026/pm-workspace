# Competitive Monitor — Full Set
**Date:** April 13, 2026
**Competitors:** Visier, Workday Analytics, SAP SuccessFactors, Tableau, Power BI, Looker, UKG, Ceridian/Dayforce

---

**Visier** — *general*

- **What changed:** Launched MCP (Model Context Protocol) for AI agents, enabling Visier's governed people data to connect into other enterprise systems and cross-functional AI workflows. Also launched a new Global Partner Program.
- **Signal strength:** High — MCP positions Visier as an infrastructure layer inside broader enterprise AI stacks, not just a standalone analytics product.
- **PM implication:** Direct threat to standalone people analytics positioning. If Visier's data layer gets embedded in other enterprise tools, switching costs go up and the moat deepens. Any product you're building that relies on proprietary data access needs to account for this.

---

**Workday Analytics** — *general*

- **What changed:** Launched "Sana" — a new AI product described as "superintelligence for work" that finds answers, takes action, and automates workflows. 2026 R1 release (460+ features) added Workday Data Cloud with zero-copy data sharing to Databricks, BigQuery, Salesforce, and Snowflake. New Self-Service Agent replacing Workday Assistant.
- **Signal strength:** High — Sana is a major strategic reposition, not an incremental feature.
- **PM implication:** Workday is moving from analytics tool to autonomous decision layer. If you're selling against Workday in HCM analytics, the "they don't do real analytics" argument is weakening fast. Workday Data Cloud also erodes the case for third-party BI integrations.

---

**SAP SuccessFactors** — *analytics*

- **What changed:** 1H 2026 release (preview live April 13, GA May 15) adds AI-Assisted Performance Insights across all review step types, new People Intelligence data products for onboarding/recruiting/learning, and EU Pay Transparency Insights.
- **Signal strength:** Medium — steady execution, nothing breakout. EU Pay Transparency is compliance-driven but opens a wedge into analytics conversations with EU-exposed enterprises.
- **PM implication:** EU Pay Transparency Insights is worth watching if your users operate globally. SAP will use compliance requirements as an analytics wedge — a playbook worth borrowing.

---

**Tableau** — *product/positioning*

- **What changed:** Launched "Concierge" (natural language Q&A), Semantic Learning for AI agents, Dynamic Spatial Parameters, and Performance Insights Dashboard. AWS Private Link support added for Cloud customers.
- **Signal strength:** Medium — natural language Q&A is now table stakes in BI; Tableau is catching up, not leading.
- **PM implication:** Tableau is incrementally closing the self-serve gap. Not a breakout move, but signals that the "Tableau is too technical" positioning is getting harder to sustain for competitors.

---

**Power BI** — *product*

- **What changed:** Translytical task flows now GA (users can take action directly from reports). New PBIR file format becoming default. Direct Lake in OneLake now GA. Copilot enhancements across Feb and March releases. Scorecards heatmap and hierarchies being removed April 15.
- **Signal strength:** High — translytical is a meaningful shift. Writing data back from a BI report is a capability most analytics tools don't have.
- **PM implication:** Power BI is blurring the line between analytics and workflow. If your product is in the Microsoft ecosystem or competes with embedded BI use cases, this is a gap to address. The Scorecards feature cuts signal noise.

---

**Looker** — *positioning*

- **What changed:** Google rebranded Looker Studio back to "Data Studio" on April 10, 2026, repositioning it as a unified hub for BigQuery agents, Colab data apps, and traditional reports — not just dashboards. Conversational Analytics added Fast and Thinking modes.
- **Signal strength:** Medium — rebrand signals a strategic pivot more than a product leap, but consolidating data assets under one surface is a real platform play.
- **PM implication:** Google is turning Looker/Data Studio into a data platform entry point rather than a standalone BI tool. If you're competing for enterprise analytics buyers in Google Cloud shops, the competitive surface just got broader.

---

**UKG** — *general*

- **What changed:** Named overall Leader in Nucleus 2026 Enterprise HCM Value Matrix (10th consecutive year for Gartner midmarket). New Workforce Intelligence Hub with real-time analytics across scheduling, time, hiring, pay, and performance. Partnership with Ellucian (higher ed ERP).
- **Signal strength:** Medium — Workforce Intelligence Hub is a meaningful product consolidation, but no major new capability.
- **PM implication:** UKG continues to win on breadth and retention. The Ellucian partnership is a vertical expansion into higher ed — worth monitoring if that's a segment you're targeting.

---

**Dayforce (formerly Ceridian)** — *general*

- **What changed:** Thoma Bravo completed $12.3B acquisition February 4, 2026. Launched AI Workspace with People Analytics Agents (role-based insights across HR, pay, time, talent), Pay Clarity agents, and Strategic Workforce Planning (AI-powered scenario modeling on live org data).
- **Signal strength:** High — PE acquisition at $12.3B signals aggressive growth intent. AI Workspace with agentic people analytics is a direct move into Visier's territory.
- **PM implication:** Dayforce under Thoma Bravo will likely accelerate M&A and product investment. Strategic Workforce Planning is a direct play in the analytics value chain. Watch for pricing pressure and faster feature velocity.

---

**So what:** The biggest signal across the set is that HCM platforms (Workday with Sana, Dayforce with AI Workspace, UKG with Workforce Intelligence Hub) are all converging on agentic analytics — moving from "show me the data" to "take action on the data." Standalone people analytics vendors like Visier are responding with infrastructure plays (MCP). For a PM in this space, the strategic question is where your product sits on that spectrum — and whether your differentiation is data access, governance, or workflow integration.
