# Competitive Snapshot — Workday Analytics
*Date: 2026-04-17*

**What they do:** Workday provides enterprise HCM and financial management software with embedded analytics — reporting, dashboards, and people analytics built into the same platform as HR, payroll, and finance data.

**Who they target:** Large enterprises (2,000+ employees), CHRO and CFO buyers, industries with complex workforce and compliance requirements (healthcare, financial services, higher ed, government).

**Differentiators (top 3):**
1. **Single data model:** HR, payroll, and finance data live in one system — no ETL, no integration lag. Analytics reflect real-time transactional data, which standalone BI tools can't replicate without connectors.
2. **Embedded compliance and security:** Role-based access, audit trails, and data governance are built in — not bolted on. Particularly strong for regulated industries.
3. **Prism Analytics:** Allows customers to blend external data sources with Workday data inside the platform, reducing dependency on external BI tools like Tableau or Power BI.

**Pricing model:** Not publicly disclosed. Enterprise contracts, typically multi-year. Prism Analytics is an add-on module priced separately. Total cost is high — commonly cited as a barrier for mid-market.

**One weakness:** Analytics UX is built for administrators and analysts, not business users. HR leaders and managers frequently report that self-serve reporting requires too much training or IT involvement. This is the gap Visier and embedded people analytics tools exploit — they offer the same data story with far less friction for the non-technical buyer.
